Buying communications infrastructure is never a solo decision — and that's where most vendor websites quietly fail.

Three very different people have to say yes, and they want opposite things:

The developer builds the integration. They're allergic to stock photos of smiling people in headsets ("this is for middle management, not me") and want native SDKs and a terminal command visible in the first ten seconds. For them, the code is the marketing.

The product manager cares about time to market. They're hunting for concrete, proven use cases — "two-factor auth for fintech," "appointment reminders for healthcare" — and proof your API turns an eight-week build into a two-week one.

The CTO is doing brutal math on uptime SLAs, unit economics, and hidden costs — pass-through fees, messaging-compliance registration, relay bandwidth. A fraction of a cent per message reshapes their balance sheet.

A single dated, vague website manages to lose all three at once. Worse, when the CTO sees an outdated storefront, they assume the security and routing are equally neglected — and they leave for a competitor who speaks their language.

The fix isn't a new coat of paint. It's a focused, developer-first experience and radical transparency about how — and where — you route.

That's the direction FoneAPI is building toward.
