# LinkedIn Image Theme Brief — iText.io (blog-001)

## Product/service summary
iText.io text-enables a business's existing phone line, turning the landline or toll-free number customers already know into a two-way SMS/RCS channel — replies centralize in a shared console while voice calls keep ringing the desk phone normally.

## Target audience
SMB and mid-market operators, customer-facing teams, and service businesses that rely on a known phone number and want to meet customers via text.

## Blog topic summary
"Why Customers Ignore Your Call but Read Your Text" — the synchronous/asynchronous channel mismatch, why text layers onto (not replaces) email, why personal phones are the wrong fix, how SPID routing text-enables a landline, what RCS changes, and the compliance bar.

## Visual theme direction
Educational, opportunity-focused, optimistic. Metaphors: a familiar phone line gaining a text channel; "voice in one lane, text in a digital bike lane alongside the highway"; an unread call vs. an opened message; a single recognizable business number bridging to a modern inbox.

## Mood and tone
Enthusiastic, practical, future-facing, clear. Approachable and modern, not cold-corporate.

## Suggested color direction
Inferred from the brand's optimistic, communication-focused feel (not confirmed exact brand hex): clean, modern palette — white/light base with a confident primary (blue or teal) and a friendly accent (green for "delivered/read"). Treat as direction, not brand-exact.

## Suggested composition
Horizontal 1.91:1 (1200x627). One clear focal concept, generous space for a headline, uncluttered.

## What the image should communicate
Keep the phone number you've always promoted — and add the text channel customers actually read.

## What to avoid
No invented logos, no real product UI/screens of iText.io, no real phone numbers, no customer names, no metrics, no certifications. Avoid generating an actual logo. Avoid readable text inside message bubbles. Avoid spammy or cluttered messaging-app imagery.

## Text handling
Avoid readable text inside the image (including legible message-bubble text or phone numbers). Leave clean space for a headline/CTA to be added manually later.
