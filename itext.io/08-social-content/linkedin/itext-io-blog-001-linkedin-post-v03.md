Combine app-like messaging power with a near-universal open rate and you get a marketer's dream — and an intensely policed channel. The text inbox is intimate, and a spam text generates far more hostility than a spam email. This is exactly where buyers are most under-educated.

A few realities to internalize before sending a single campaign:

TCPA penalties are severe — fines can reach up to roughly $1,500 per unauthorized message. And consent is nuanced: informational texts (an appointment reminder, a reply to an inquiry) can rest on implied consent, but promotional texts require explicit written opt-in. Buying a product doesn't grant marketing consent.

Carriers gate access through 10DLC registration — essentially TSA pre-check for texting. Skip it and your throughput gets throttled or blocked. "Instant setup" only ever means the dashboard turns on, not that you're cleared to send.

Algorithms watch content in real time. Public link shorteners get blocked; "SHAFT" categories get shut down without age-gating.

Regulated industries need more than SMS. Standard SMS is unencrypted, so healthcare and similar fields must use secure pathways and confirm their vendor will sign a BAA.

The inbox is a sacred space — businesses have to earn the right to be there.
