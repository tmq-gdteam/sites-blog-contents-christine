Your phone is probably within arm's reach right now. If it rang from an unknown business number, there's a good chance you'd let it go to voicemail. But if that same business texted you, you'd almost certainly read it within minutes.

That gap isn't a quirk of your habits. It's a structural mismatch between the channel businesses prefer — the synchronous phone call, which demands both people be present at once — and the asynchronous channels customers actually respond to.

The engagement data explains why texting wins. Commonly cited figures put SMS open rates around 98%, with most messages read within minutes, and the average text reply coming back in roughly 90 seconds versus closer to 90 minutes for email. The reason is human: text doesn't demand someone's immediate, undivided attention. It hands control back to the customer.

This isn't about abandoning the phone or email — it's strategic layering. Email still wins for dense, secure documents; a text is what cuts through the noise to say "we just sent it."

If your team is still chasing customers by phone and losing them to voicemail, the opportunity is to meet them in the channel they already prefer — without throwing away the number you've promoted for years.
