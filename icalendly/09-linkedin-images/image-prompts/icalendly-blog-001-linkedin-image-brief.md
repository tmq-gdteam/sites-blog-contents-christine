# LinkedIn Image Theme Brief — iCalendly (blog-001)

## Product/service summary
iCalendly is an AI-powered scheduling platform that books meetings and then proactively confirms, reminds, follows up, and recovers missed appointments across phone, text, and email — an "attentive host" rather than a passive booking link.

## Target audience
Sales, revenue, and operations leaders; healthcare practices and service businesses that lose revenue to no-shows and slow lead response.

## Blog topic summary
"The $150 Billion Ghost" — why ~23% of booked meetings vanish, the brutal math of speed-to-lead, why SMS beats email for reminders, and the compliance + human-handoff parts teams get wrong.

## Visual theme direction
Modern SaaS, productivity-forward. Metaphors: a calendar with one slot "kept" and others fading/empty; the "attentive host" vs. "unattended podium"; proactive reminders bridging a gap between a booking and a filled room; multi-channel (phone/text/email) converging on a confirmed meeting.

## Mood and tone
Confident, energetic, polished, optimistic, solution-oriented. Not anxious or guilt-driven.

## Suggested color direction
Inferred from the brand's modern, energetic SaaS feel (not confirmed exact brand hex): clean white/neutral base with a vibrant primary accent (blue or violet) and a warm secondary accent (green/coral) for "confirmed/attended." Treat as direction, not brand-exact.

## Suggested composition
Horizontal 1.91:1 (1200x627). Bright, airy, generous negative space for a headline. One clear focal concept; uncluttered.

## What the image should communicate
Bridging the gap between "booked" and "attended" — proactive, friendly follow-through that recovers meetings.

## What to avoid
No invented logos, no real brand UI/screens of iCalendly, no customer names, no metrics, no certifications. Avoid generating an actual logo. Avoid readable text inside the image. Avoid clichéd sad/ghost imagery played literally; keep it professional and positive.

## Text handling
Avoid readable text inside the image. Leave clean space for a headline/CTA to be added manually later.
