A 47-hour reply against a five-minute window isn't a slow race. It's not the same sport.

Here's the tension most teams live with. Reach a new inbound lead within the first five minutes — the "golden window" — and they're far more likely to qualify than a lead contacted just half an hour later. Wait past ~10 minutes and conversion can fall off a cliff. Yet the average B2B company takes somewhere around 42–47 hours to respond, and many never respond at all.

Why so steep? Context. When someone submits a form, the tab is open and intent is live. Minutes later they've answered a Slack, walked the dog, or opened a competitor's site. Dragging their attention back is enormously harder.

But speed alone isn't the whole answer — the channel matters just as much. Automated email opens around 20–30%; text opens near 98%, with interactive response rates far higher. That interactivity is the unlock for the anxious no-show, too: it's much easier to text "R" to reschedule than to psych yourself up for a phone call.

Human teams can't hit a five-minute window at scale. That's exactly the work worth automating — so your people can focus on the conversations that close.
