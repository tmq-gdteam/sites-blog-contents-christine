A great first call. You send the booking link, they pick a time, you check it off — locked in. Then 2 p.m. arrives, you're in the Zoom room, and… nothing. You've been ghosted.

It feels like a minor annoyance. At scale it's a systemic leak. The global average no-show rate sits around 23% — nearly one in four booked meetings simply evaporates. In healthcare alone, missed appointments are estimated to cost around $150 billion a year.

The hidden cause is the booking link itself. It's an open reservation book on an unattended podium: anyone can write their name, but no one stands behind it to make the meeting happen. The biggest predictor of a no-show is lead time — same-day bookings barely flake, while meetings booked weeks out abandon at roughly a third. And the status quo punishes the person most likely to cancel: when anxiety or a conflict hits, digging up the confirmation email to find a tiny cancel link is harder than just disappearing.

The fix isn't a prettier link. It's active connective tissue between "booked" and "attended" — proactive, low-friction reminders on the channels people actually read.

If a quarter of your meetings are quietly slipping away, that gap is where to start.
