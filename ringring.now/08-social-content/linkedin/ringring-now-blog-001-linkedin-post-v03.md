If you're evaluating real-time voice translation for sales, support, or negotiation, here's what actually matters — beyond the demo:

1. Latency under ~1 second. That's the threshold where a mediated exchange still feels like a conversation. Test it on a normal network, not just a staged demo, because real-world conditions are where it slips.

2. How it handles your terminology. The risk isn't everyday chat — it's a confidently mistranslated legal or financial term. Ask how the system manages domain-specific vocabulary, and treat "perfect accuracy" claims with skepticism.

3. Voice authenticity you can hear. Don't take "100% your voice" on faith. Look for a hear-it-yourself sample — source clip, then the same voice in another language — ideally without handing over your email first.

4. Data guarantees, front and center. For confidential calls, you need an explicit, visible commitment that conversations aren't retained to train public models — not a buried policy.

5. The right benchmark. Compare it to a human interpreter, not just other software. It has to be faster, cheaper, and trustworthy enough that going without a human feels like a gain.
