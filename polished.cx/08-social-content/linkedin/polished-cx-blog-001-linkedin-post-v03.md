If you're evaluating real-time voice AI, the capabilities are only half the diligence. The other half is risk. A few questions separate serious vendors from the rest:

What happens to voice data? A precise voice map can function as a blueprint for a deepfake, and under rules like GDPR it may be treated as biometric data. Ask whether any playable audio is ever stored — the safer answer is no.

How is latency actually measured? Push past "zero latency" marketing; it's a myth given the physics of data crossing the globe. Ask for real numbers against the ~150ms benchmark for natural conversation.

How is quality verified? "Sounds clear to me" panels aren't enough. Mature vendors use objective, algorithmic metrics that prove the system preserves a speaker's identity, not just smooths noise.

Where are the hard limits? In healthcare, for example, voice AI may suit scheduling and billing inquiries — but not clinical diagnosis or replacing a certified medical interpreter.

Even if you never sign a contract, these questions protect your people, your customers, and your compliance posture.
