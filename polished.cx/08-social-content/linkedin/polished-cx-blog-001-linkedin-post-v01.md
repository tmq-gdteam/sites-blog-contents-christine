"Sorry, can you repeat that?"

It feels like a tiny moment. But repeated across millions of calls, that pause has a name — acoustic friction — and a measurable cost.

UK contact center analysis suggests a single 15-second repetition eats roughly 4.2% of an average call. Scale that across billions of calls and one industry estimate puts the annual drain near £143M (about $170M).

The deeper cost is human. A half-heard conversation is unpredictable, and the brain can't stop trying to fill the gap — quietly draining the same cognitive battery that powers focus and patience. It's a real reason contact center burnout and "Zoom fatigue" aren't just complaints about long shifts.

The encouraging part: real-time voice AI can now reduce that friction — localizing accents, cutting noise, and translating speech while keeping the speaker's natural tone and emotion intact. The principle that matters most is clarity without erasure: remove the friction, not the person's identity.

If your team loses time and energy to "can you repeat that?" every day, it's worth understanding what modern voice AI can — and can't — do about it.
