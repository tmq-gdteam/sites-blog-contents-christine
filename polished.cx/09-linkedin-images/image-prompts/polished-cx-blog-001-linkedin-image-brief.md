# LinkedIn Image Theme Brief — Polished.CX (blog-001)

## Product/service summary
Polished.CX is a real-time voice AI layer for live conversations. It localizes accents, removes background noise, translates across languages, and preserves the speaker's natural voice identity — positioned around "enhance, don't erase."

## Target audience
Enterprise CX and contact center leaders, global BPO decision-makers, and voice-product teams; secondary: healthcare, sales, EdTech, and remote teams.

## Blog topic summary
"The Hidden Cost of Acoustic Friction" — how the small moment of "can you repeat that?" creates measurable financial and cognitive cost, the ethics of accent localization vs. erasure, and what makes real-time voice AI trustworthy (latency, privacy, quality, limits).

## Visual theme direction
Clean, editorial, enterprise-tech. Visual metaphors for clarity emerging from noise: sound waves resolving from chaotic to smooth, a clear voice channel cutting through distortion, abstract audio waveforms. Human-centered without identifiable real people.

## Mood and tone
Confident, calm, premium, human-centered, futuristic. Not alarmist, not cluttered.

## Suggested color direction
Inferred from the brand's confident, futuristic, enterprise feel (not confirmed exact brand hex codes): deep navy/indigo base with a clean teal or electric-blue accent and generous white/neutral space. Keep it cool, modern, and uncluttered. Treat as direction, not a brand-exact palette.

## Suggested composition
Horizontal 1.91:1 (1200x627) for LinkedIn feed. Strong negative space on one side for headline text to be added later. Single clear focal metaphor; avoid busy diagrams.

## What the image should communicate
Clarity, signal over noise, and preserving the human voice — communication made effortless without flattening identity.

## What to avoid
No invented logos, brand marks, certifications, UI screens, dashboards, customer names, awards, or metrics. No fake people that imply named customers. Avoid long readable text inside the image. Avoid robotic/dystopian or "accent removal as erasure" connotations. Avoid clichéd headset call-center stock imagery if possible.

## Text handling
Avoid generating readable text inside the image. Leave clean space so any headline/CTA can be added manually after generation.
