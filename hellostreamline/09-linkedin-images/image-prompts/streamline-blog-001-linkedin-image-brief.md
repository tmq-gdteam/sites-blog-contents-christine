# LinkedIn Image Theme Brief — Streamline / hellostreamline (blog-001)

## Product/service summary
Streamline is a "cognitive interaction operating system" — an AI orchestration layer that sits between front-end channels (SMS, voice, video, social) and back-end systems (CRM, ERP, logistics), routing conversations, automating workflows, and preserving customer context so people don't repeat themselves and teams stop drowning in tabs.

## Target audience
Enterprise IT, operations, and CX leaders dealing with SaaS sprawl, fragmented channels, and broken customer context.

## Blog topic summary
"Why Buying More Software Is Quietly Making Your Team Slower" — digital gridlock from ~101 SaaS apps and constant context switching, what AI orchestration (and MCP) actually does, the risk of centralization, and the compliance bar to demand.

## Visual theme direction
Ambitious, operational, enterprise-infrastructure. Metaphors: tangled tools/wires becoming one clean orchestrated flow; an air-traffic-controller/hub connecting many channels; scattered app tiles unifying into a single intelligent layer; "many remotes" vs. one brain.

## Mood and tone
Confident, intelligent, calm-amid-complexity, premium enterprise. Order emerging from chaos — not busy or overwhelming.

## Suggested color direction
Inferred from the brand's broad, futuristic, infrastructure-minded feel (not confirmed exact brand hex): deep blue/indigo base with a bright connective accent (cyan or violet) and clean neutrals; sense of a connected network. Treat as direction, not brand-exact.

## Suggested composition
Horizontal 1.91:1 (1200x627). A central hub with orderly connections; generous negative space for a headline; clean, not cluttered.

## What the image should communicate
Orchestration over accumulation — an intelligent layer that connects fragmented tools and channels into one coherent flow.

## What to avoid
No invented logos, no real third-party SaaS brand marks or UI, no customer names, no metrics, no certifications. Avoid generating an actual logo. Avoid readable text. Avoid an overly chaotic "wire spaghetti" image that reads as messy rather than resolved.

## Text handling
Avoid readable text inside the image. Leave clean space for a headline/CTA to be added manually later.
