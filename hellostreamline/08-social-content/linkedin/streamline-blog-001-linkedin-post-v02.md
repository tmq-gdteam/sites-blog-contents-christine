You've felt this without naming it. You reply to an automated shipping text with a question, then call support — and the agent can't see your text, because it's locked in the marketing silo. So you start over, repeating your account number for the fifth time.

That's not just an annoyance. It's architectural siloing: the marketing team's SMS tool, the logistics team's tracking software, and the support team's phone system were never designed with each other in mind, so the data gets permanently trapped in isolated databases. The left hand doesn't know what the right hand is doing, and the customer pays for it.

The emerging fix is AI orchestration — a "brain" that sits between your front-end channels (SMS, voice, video, social) and your back-end systems (CRM, ERP, logistics). The technical unlock is the Model Context Protocol: instead of brittle, hand-coded API connectors, an AI agent reads an unfamiliar system's structure on the fly and knows how to ask for what it needs. Like giving it a universal-translator earpiece instead of a hard-coded phrasebook.

So a customer texts "where's my order?", the system interprets intent, pulls the tracking data, and replies — no human keystroke, no app to download.

The shift is from accumulation to orchestration.
