# LinkedIn Image Theme Brief — halfoff.ai (blog-001)

## Product/service summary
halfoff.ai rebuilds the SaaS platforms companies already use on owned, managed open-source infrastructure, migrates the data with minimal downtime, and runs the replacement stack for a lower recurring cost — so customers keep the code and data.

## Target audience
CFOs, finance leaders, and IT/engineering decision-makers frustrated by rising SaaS costs and vendor lock-in.

## Blog topic summary
"The SaaS Lobster Trap" — why software bills keep rising (AI bundling, opaque credits), why optimization tools only trim the rent, and the realistic escape route of managed-open-source repatriation, including the legal and migration catches.

## Visual theme direction
Defiant, CFO-friendly, challenger-brand. Metaphors: escaping a trap/cage; rising cost curve vs. flat owned cost; "rent vs. own" (key/ownership); a door that's actually open. Financial-but-modern, not stuffy.

## Mood and tone
Provocative, confident, pragmatic, sharp. Empowering ("the door is open"), not gloomy.

## Suggested color direction
Inferred from the brand's defiant, financially sharp, engineering-led feel (not confirmed exact brand hex): dark or high-contrast base with a bold accent (electric green or amber suggesting savings/value) and clean neutrals. Treat as direction, not brand-exact.

## Suggested composition
Horizontal 1.91:1 (1200x627). One strong focal metaphor, generous negative space for a headline. Clean, modern, confident.

## What the image should communicate
Breaking free from inflating, locked-in software costs toward owned, predictable infrastructure.

## What to avoid
No invented logos (yours or competitors'), no real SaaS brand marks or UI, no customer names, no fabricated metrics or savings figures inside the image, no certifications. Avoid readable text. Avoid literal money-pile clichés if possible; keep it strategic and modern.

## Text handling
Avoid readable text inside the image. Leave clean space for a headline to be added manually later.
