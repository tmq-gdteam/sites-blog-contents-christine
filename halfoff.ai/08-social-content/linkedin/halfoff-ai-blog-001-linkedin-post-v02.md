Your SaaS bill went up again — and the vendor never sent an email saying "we raised your price 11% because we can." They use packaging tactics instead.

The big one right now is AI bundling, what one research brief calls the "innovation tax." Generative-AI features get folded into base plans whether you asked for them or not, doubling as cover for raising the overall price — with no way to opt out. Like a landlord installing a smart thermostat in a hallway you never walk through, then citing it to raise the rent.

The second tactic is the shift from flat pricing to opaque credit systems. Instead of buying a service, you buy a bucket of abstract tokens — and the vendor keeps the right to change how many tokens an action costs mid-contract. Your headline fee never moves, but your real cost per workflow climbs invisibly.

FinOps tools help, but they optimize the rent: they cancel leases on empty apartments without stopping the landlord from raising prices on the ones you still need. The only structural fix is to stop renting — moving core workflows onto infrastructure you own.

If your bill climbs every year for features you didn't ask for, it's worth modeling your true five-year cost of ownership.
