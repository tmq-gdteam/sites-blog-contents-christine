If you're evaluating AI for your contact center, here are five things worth doing before you sign anything:

1. Reframe the language internally. Decide now whether this is a support tool or a surveillance tool. "Live guardrails" and "supportive prompts" build adoption; "monitoring" and "mistake detection" build resentment. How a tool is framed shapes how it gets deployed.

2. Target cognitive load, not just automation rate. The fastest wins come from collapsing multiple screens into one and eliminating after-call work — that's where minutes and morale are actually recovered.

3. Treat vendor metrics as claims. Numbers like "40% faster onboarding" are goals until a documented case study in an environment like yours backs them up. A modest, defensible result beats an impressive, unverifiable one.

4. Make compliance an architecture question, not a badge. Ask how sensitive data is redacted, where it's processed, and whether it's used to train anyone's models. Never accept a trust badge a vendor can't fully prove.

5. Protect the human in the loop. The hard, emotional edge cases still need people. The tool should shield them — from abusive callers and from subjective reviews — not just watch them.
