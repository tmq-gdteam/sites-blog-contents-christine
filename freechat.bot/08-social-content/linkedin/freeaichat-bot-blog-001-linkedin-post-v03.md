Thinking about adding an AI chatbot to your small-business website? A short checklist before you do:

1. Frame it as a receptionist, not an "AI agent." Expect it to greet visitors, answer common questions (hours, pricing, booking), capture the lead, and flag you when it's urgent. Right-sized expectations mean it actually delivers.

2. Pressure-test "free." Does the free plan stay genuinely usable, or does it throttle, expire, or demand a card the moment you rely on it? A real free plan has no trial countdown.

3. Demand it sounds like you. The bot should reply in your voice, using only the facts you provide — that's your guardrail against it going off-script or inventing a discount.

4. Insist on a one-line install — and proof. "Add one line of code" terrifies non-technical owners. Look for a tool that shows the copy-paste in action, not just claims it's easy.

5. Own your data. Your leads and conversations should be yours: encrypted, deletable anytime, and not siphoned off to train global models.

The goal isn't an all-knowing assistant. It's reliably capturing the leads you already paid to attract — even at 9 PM on a Friday.
