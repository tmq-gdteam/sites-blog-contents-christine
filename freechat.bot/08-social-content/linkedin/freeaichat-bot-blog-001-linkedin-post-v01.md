Most small businesses don't lose customers because their service is bad. They lose them at 9 PM on a Friday, when a high-intent visitor lands on the website, finds a static contact form, types a question, hits submit — and hears nothing back until Monday.

Here's what makes that quietly expensive: you already paid to get that visitor there. Through SEO you optimized, or ads you ran. That's your acquisition cost, already spent. When they bounce because no one answered a simple question about pricing or availability, that's not a missed opportunity — it's revenue you paid to lose.

A static form after hours is the digital version of leaving the shop doors open, the lights off, and a "leave a note" sign on the register — while a customer stands there, cash in hand.

The fix isn't a smarter "AI agent" that promises to run your business. It's a humbler idea: a digital receptionist. Something that greets visitors, answers the common questions in your voice, captures the lead, and flags you when it's genuinely urgent. One job, done well.

That framing matters because it sets honest expectations — and it's a job AI can actually do reliably today.

If you run a small business: who's answering your website at 9 PM? Worth finding out before your next ad spend.
