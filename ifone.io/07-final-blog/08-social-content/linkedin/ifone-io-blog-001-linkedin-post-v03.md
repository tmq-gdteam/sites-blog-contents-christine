Before you connect any app to your email, messages, and calendar, here's a short trust checklist worth running:

1. Does it structure, or just aggregate? A unified inbox that's only a bigger chronological feed adds noise. Look for intelligent search that pulls the right thread, email, and meeting note together on demand. Structure is the whole point.

2. Is privacy stated in specifics? "Military-grade security" is a non-answer. You want concrete, verifiable mechanics: how data is encrypted in storage and in transit, where it's processed, and an explicit promise it isn't used to train models.

3. Are compliance claims provable? Don't take badges on faith — they should be backed by real, publicly linkable third-party audits. A single exaggeration about security is disqualifying.

4. Is the infrastructure itself maintained? A valid security certificate and an up-to-date site aren't details — they're the first evidence the team takes your data seriously.

5. Does it sound built for you, not the masses? Consumer apps where "you are the product" play by different rules than tools you pay for to protect your work.
