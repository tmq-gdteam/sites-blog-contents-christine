# LinkedIn Image Theme Brief — noforgery.ai (blog-001)

## Product/service summary
noforgery.ai is a real-time, multimodal verification system that determines whether a voice/face on a live interaction is real and belongs to who it claims — combining acoustic, visual, and linguistic signals with an edge-native, zero-retention architecture (raw biometrics never leave the device).

## Target audience
Security, fraud, risk, and identity leaders in banking/fintech, contact centers, and enterprises handling high-risk transactions and account recovery.

## Blog topic summary
"The 54% Coin Flip" — humans can barely tell real voices from AI clones; SMS OTPs and static voiceprints are obsolete; the credible defense is continuous, multimodal, privacy-preserving verification.

## Visual theme direction
Security-forward, cyber/forensic aesthetic. Visual metaphors: a real-vs-synthetic waveform comparison, an audio waveform under forensic scan, a verification "verdict" concept (real / suspect / fake) expressed abstractly, audio-visual phoneme/lip-sync alignment as abstract geometry, on-device shield/lock.

## Mood and tone
Urgent, serious, precise, high-stakes, adversarial but controlled. Trustworthy, not fear-mongering.

## Suggested color direction
Inferred from the brand's dark, cybernetic, security feel (not confirmed exact brand hex): near-black/deep charcoal base with cool blue/cyan technical accents; sparing use of red/amber for "threat" and green/teal for "verified." Treat as direction, not brand-exact.

## Suggested composition
Horizontal 1.91:1 (1200x627). Strong dark field with one focused luminous element; negative space for a headline. High contrast, sharp, technical.

## What the image should communicate
Detection, scrutiny, and trustworthy real-time verification of identity — security that is precise and privacy-respecting.

## What to avoid
No invented logos, certifications, real faces of identifiable people, UI screens, dashboards, customer names, or metrics. Avoid generating an actual brand mark. Avoid long readable text. Avoid hacker-in-a-hoodie cliché and gratuitous fear imagery. Keep biometric/face references abstract, never a recognizable real individual.

## Text handling
Avoid readable text inside the image. Leave clean dark space for a headline to be added manually later.
