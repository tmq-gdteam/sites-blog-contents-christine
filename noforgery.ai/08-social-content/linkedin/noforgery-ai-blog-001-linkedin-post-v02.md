Here's the uncomfortable part of modern fraud: the tools we trust most are the ones already broken.

The six-digit code texted to your phone feels secure. In practice it's a key under the doormat — liftable through SIM swapping and weaknesses in SS7, the aging switchboard telecoms use to route messages. Regulators are now writing that obsolescence into law, with mandates phasing out SMS OTPs for high-risk transactions.

Voice biometrics have the same problem. A static voiceprint was treated as an immutable signature — until generative AI made cloning a voice from seconds of audio cheap and convincing. Worse, a static check happens once, at "hello." Clear that gate with a legitimate sample and the system stops watching; minutes later a deepfake can authorize a transfer while the guard is asleep at an open gate.

No single signal is enough anymore. The honest fix layers acoustic forensics, audio-visual alignment, and linguistic signals — and keeps checking across the whole interaction, not just the opening second.

If you're relying on a single liveness check, that's the gap worth closing first.
