If you're evaluating deepfake-detection or voice-verification vendors, a slick architecture and a "99% accurate" slide are not proof. Three things separate a real defense from a good-looking demo:

Demand rigorous accuracy metrics, not headline percentages. "99%" is meaningless if the missing fraction locks out your CEO mid-acquisition or blocks a thousand real customers from their accounts. Mature buyers look at cost-weighted metrics that weigh the cost of letting a fake through against the cost of falsely blocking a real user.

Get cryptographic proof of the privacy promise. "Zero retention" should be backed by data-processing agreements and independent SOC 2 Type 2 attestation that raw media is genuinely destroyed on-device — not a line written by marketing.

Insist on a human in the loop. The technology should act as a decision-support layer that escalates suspect cases, never an autonomous black box that permanently locks people out.

A vendor confident in its architecture should welcome those questions.
