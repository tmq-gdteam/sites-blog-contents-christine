Asking a visitor to fill an eight-field form is like telling them to eat a whole cake in one bite. Most won't.

That's why conversational lead capture converts better — the mechanism is progressive profiling. A chatbot serves the same questions one bite at a time. "How can I help today?" The visitor explains their problem. "Got it — what's the best email to send this to?" Each small answer is a micro-commitment, and someone who's invested in the first two is far more willing to share a phone number on the fourth message than they'd ever have been seeing all four fields up front.

Completion rates climb from the low double digits for forms to as high as ~70% for conversational flows. And it doesn't cannibalize what works: when a site offers both, form-lovers keep using forms while chat catches the high-intent visitors who would otherwise have bounced.

Two cautions before you turn one on: ground it in your own content with retrieval and guardrails so it can't invent answers, and handle consent and data deletion properly. A vendor serious about protecting you will have clear answers; a 30-second setup that glosses over all of it is the red flag.
