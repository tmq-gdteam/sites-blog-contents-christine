Imagine a beautiful flagship store. Premium lighting, a line out the door on opening day. Customers walk in, love the products — and find no employees anywhere. Just a clipboard demanding their name, phone, and a detailed description of what they want before anyone will speak to them.

You'd fire that manager on the spot. Yet that's almost exactly how most businesses operate online.

The numbers are brutal: of every 100 visitors to an average business site, only around 1.4% become captured leads. The rest look around and leave without a trace — many of whom actually wanted to buy.

The main culprit is cognitive load. A high-intent visitor hits a page with eight empty form fields and their effort spikes, along with privacy anxiety. It stops feeling like a transaction and starts feeling like an interrogation — long-form B2B demo requests abandon around 80%. The phone fallback fails too: small businesses miss a large share of inbound calls, and most callers who hit voicemail just hang up.

The fix isn't a better form. It's turning the first moment into a conversation instead of an interrogation — meeting the speed-to-lead reality a busy owner physically can't.
